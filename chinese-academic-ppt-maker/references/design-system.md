# Design System

## Layout

- Use restrained academic layouts with clear hierarchy.
- Put one main claim at the top.
- Put evidence in the middle: table, figure, workflow, or compact diagram.
- Put interpretation at the bottom or in speaker notes.
- Avoid card-heavy marketing layouts for scientific reports.
- Avoid decorative gradients, blobs, and oversized hero sections.

## Typography

- Use one Chinese-capable sans-serif font when possible: Microsoft YaHei, DengXian, SimHei, Source Han Sans, or Noto Sans CJK.
- Slide titles: 26-34 pt.
- Body text: 16-22 pt.
- Figure/table labels: 10-14 pt only when legible.
- Do not use long paragraphs. Split or move to notes.

## Color

Use semantic colors consistently:

- Treatment / intervention: muted coral or red.
- Control / usual care: steel blue.
- Benefit / verified: muted green.
- Warning / open issue: amber.
- Neutral structure: charcoal, gray, white.

Avoid one-note palettes dominated by a single hue.

## Tables

Use tables for comparison, not storage.

Good table columns:

- `问题`
- `当前结论`
- `证据`
- `待确认`

For numeric result tables, always include cohort/split labels.

## Figures

Prefer real project figures. If figures are too dense:

- crop to the relevant panel;
- add a short caption;
- create a companion explanation slide;
- do not redraw scientific plots unless asked.

## Speaker Notes

Use notes for:

- definitions the user may forget;
- cautious口径;
- anticipated questions;
- what not to overstate.

Do not put "how to present this slide" instructions visibly on the slide.
